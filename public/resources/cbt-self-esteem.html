CBT: What is Self‑Esteem?

Self‑esteem is the opinion we have about ourselves. In CBT, we explore the beliefs that shape self‑esteem and learn practical ways to build a kinder, more balanced view of ourselves.

How low self‑esteem forms

  • Early experiences (criticism, comparison, bullying)
  • Unhelpful rules ("I must never make mistakes")
  • Negative automatic thoughts ("I’m not good enough")

CBT tools to improve self‑esteem

  • Evidence gathering: List daily examples that contradict harsh self‑judgments.
  • Balanced thinking: Replace all‑or‑nothing thoughts with realistic alternatives.
  • Behavioral experiments: Test beliefs by trying small, safe challenges.
  • Compassionate self‑talk: Speak to yourself as you would to a close friend.

Daily practice

  • 3 evidence items supporting a balanced view of you
  • 1 small action that aligns with your values
  • 1 kind sentence you would say to a friend—say it to yourself
